Deliver: samplers for all families sufficient to reproduce atlas behavior; SGP4 worker provider and pre-sampled SPICE provider; `argelander-leaflet` painting strips with all six treatments and the trail-decay canvas; the demo-leaflet app with live SGP4 footprints over open tiles; three demonstration configurations (an Earth SAR constellation, ICESat-2 beads, a Mars orbiter pair CTX plus HiRISE targeting) proving the planetary path early; atlas tiles re-rendered through argelander-core, converting the demo into the regression corpus.

Exit: golden-image tests per family against the re-rendered atlas; AGE-15 budget measured and recorded; antimeridian and polar cases tested (AGE-10).

## Demo state (post slice 6, iterated live through 2026-07-11)

The demo at /demo/ on Pages has grown well past its one-line deliverable and now proves engine claims the specs only assert. It flies nine satellites with twelve toggleable instruments from fresh Celestrak elements (ISS with a demo whiskbroom and GEDI beads, Terra with MODIS and tasked ASTER scenes, Sentinel-1A with tasked IW slots, Landsat 9, SWOT split into KaRIn twins and Poseidon-3C, NISAR SweepSAR, MetOp-C ASCAT twins, Sentinel-6A, ICESat-2), all propagated in a worker-hosted Sgp4Provider through the port seam so the main thread only paints (AGE-05 end to end). All six treatments render by the atlas recipes under the atlas names, on three switchable basemaps with the dark ground default; the plan executes on the clock (states re-emit per segment boundary via withStateRule, tasked windows are separate strips sharing a passId with honest gaps, the trail resets when the pass loops); the AGE-09 hybrid reveals scan mechanisms by projected width. Field-tested in a real browser through eight fix rounds; the regressions that had guards addable got them.

API this hardened, which Phase 2 MMGIS work builds on rather than reinvents: core trackStrip (symmetric swath, scan, offset ribbon, beads, plus offsetRangeKm) and withStateRule (the AGE-13 scrub primitive); adapter AcquisitionLayer.updateStates (state re-emission without a view or trail reset), TREATMENT_LABELS, paintGuide, the per-instrument one-layer-each posture (the AGE-12 configuration shape), and the projector contract that already survives integer-rounding hosts. Still simulated and labeled as such: committed means predicted until the AGE-04 telemetry bridge; the pointed and scanning families stay out of the demo until slice 5 bridges their samplers to live tracks, absent rather than faked.

## Planetary and polar state (landed 2026-07-12)

The Mars CTX plus HiRISE pair is delivered, and the Moon rode along: the demo grew a world control (Earth, Moon, Mars), each world owning its map and CRS (plate carree L.CRS.EPSG4326 over NASA Trek tiles off Earth), with MRO flying CTX continuous plus HiRISE tasked scenes and LRO flying LROC WAC plus LOLA beads, both served as demonstration state tables through PresampledProvider so the planetary seam is proven end to end and nothing downstream can tell the pre-sampled path from the SGP4 worker. The polar half of the AGE-10 exit criterion is formal: the polar suite crosses both poles with real states and asserts finite paints, held great-circle swath width, bounded footprints, and unribboned beads; no engine change was needed. The developer guide (docs/configuring-layers.md, reviewed by the three-lens adversarial workflow twice) carries Earth, Moon, and Mars minimal examples. Of the three demonstration configurations of the deliverable line, all are now closed: SAR constellation and ICESat-2 beads by the live Earth constellation, the Mars pair by the worlds. Antimeridian cases were tested in the world-copy fix rounds; the remaining exit items are the golden-image tests, the AGE-15 budget measurement, and the atlas re-render.
