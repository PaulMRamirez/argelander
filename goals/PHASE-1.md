## Demo state (post slice 6, iterated live through 2026-07-11)

The demo at /demo/ on Pages has grown well past its one-line deliverable and now proves engine claims the specs only assert. It flies nine satellites with twelve toggleable instruments from fresh Celestrak elements (ISS with a demo whiskbroom and GEDI beads, Terra with MODIS and tasked ASTER scenes, Sentinel-1A with tasked IW slots, Landsat 9, SWOT split into KaRIn twins and Poseidon-3C, NISAR SweepSAR, MetOp-C ASCAT twins, Sentinel-6A, ICESat-2), all propagated in a worker-hosted Sgp4Provider through the port seam so the main thread only paints (AGE-05 end to end). All six treatments render by the atlas recipes under the atlas names, on three switchable basemaps with the dark ground default; the plan executes on the clock (states re-emit per segment boundary via withStateRule, tasked windows are separate strips sharing a passId with honest gaps, the trail resets when the pass loops); the AGE-09 hybrid reveals scan mechanisms by projected width. Field-tested in a real browser through eight fix rounds; the regressions that had guards addable got them.

API this hardened, which Phase 2 MMGIS work builds on rather than reinvents: core trackStrip (symmetric swath, scan, offset ribbon, beads, plus offsetRangeKm) and withStateRule (the AGE-13 scrub primitive); adapter AcquisitionLayer.updateStates (state re-emission without a view or trail reset), TREATMENT_LABELS, paintGuide, the per-instrument one-layer-each posture (the AGE-12 configuration shape), and the projector contract that already survives integer-rounding hosts. Still simulated and labeled as such: committed means predicted until the AGE-04 telemetry bridge; the pointed and scanning families stay out of the demo until slice 5 bridges their samplers to live tracks, absent rather than faked.

## The radar mechanisms plumbed (landed 2026-07-12)

The radar and scatterometer families were in the demo as envelopes only, plain offset or bilateral ribbons with no mechanism, because the painter rendered every sub-structure kind except sub-swath and trackStrip had no posture to emit the banded quilt. Closed on both counts: the painter gained a strip-level sub-swath pass drawing along-track dividers for a wide swath carrying two or more sub-swaths (the SweepSAR digital-beam receive quilt), while a single hopping sub-swath per segment stays a burst whose quads already break through the connection rule (the ScanSAR/TOPS azimuth quilt). trackStrip gained subSwaths (count, optional burstPeriodSec) and looks (azimuthsRad, the fan-beam fore/mid/aft looks), both decorating a swath and forwarded through passStrips onto both sides of a bilateral pair. NISAR now flies six SweepSAR DBF beams, Sentinel-1 C-SAR its TOPS bursts across three IW sub-swaths, and ASCAT its fan-beam looks on twin swaths, all field-tested.

## Golden corpus and the performance budget (landed 2026-07-12)

Two of the three remaining exit items are closed; the third, the atlas re-render, is deferred by choice, and the reason belongs in the ledger. AGE-17 asked for a golden-image visual test per family. The corpus is the engine's own draw program, not the atlas tiles re-projected: each family renders under mechanism into the recording context that the painter tests already use, and the ordered fills and strokes with the hue, alpha, width, dash, and composite in force at each are snapshotted as the golden, with the pushbroom anchor additionally locked under all six treatments so the treatment styling is pinned too. A trace is the image without a rasterizer, so the golden stays a vector, deterministic and diffable, and no canvas dependency crosses into the tests, which keeps the adapter within the zero-dependency charter and needs no ADR. The numeric half of AGE-17 was already standing: fixtures.test and the sampler round-trips pin every family's strip geometry against its model core-side. Per-treatment behavior on the non-anchor families stays asserted directly in paint.test (hue counts, quad connectivity, dashes, burst breaks), so the 21-by-6 cross-product was not worth re-locking the same geometry six times; the corpus is 27 goldens, not 126.

The atlas re-render is deliberately not done. Making the engine reproduce the atlas tiles pixel-for-pixel would turn the atlas into a sync target, and the atlas is more valuable as a hand-authored exploration surface that can drift ahead of or behind the engine while it teaches the 21 families. The golden corpus gives the regression lock the re-render was meant to provide, sourced from the engine itself, so the exit criterion's intent is met without binding the two artifacts together.

AGE-15 sets a 60 fps budget with 20 animated layers and asks LOD to degrade gracefully before the frame rate does. Both halves are now closed, one by proof and one by measurement. The degradation lever is proven deterministically, not by timing: below its width gate a strip paints its envelope rather than its mechanism, and the envelope emits strictly fewer draw primitives, so every swath-bearing family (all but the pure bead and point geometries) sheds work by primitive count before it could shed a frame; the test selects that population by projected width and asserts the inequality over it, so a family that stopped simplifying fails rather than being skipped. The per-frame cost is measured as the JS the adapter spends walking twenty layers into a recording context on a full repaint, the slice AGE owns; the recorder counts its own bookkeeping alongside the walk, so the figure bounds the adapter's JS from above rather than isolating it. On an Apple M4 Pro the worst case, all twenty in mechanism, runs a median near 0.9 ms, with the p95 landing in a 1.2 to 1.9 ms band across repeated runs, roughly eighteen times inside the 16.67 ms frame, so the engine's contribution to the budget has a wide margin. The median is the reproducible datum; the budget test's ceiling is deliberately loose, an order-of-magnitude guard against a frame miss rather than a lock on the datum drifting a millisecond. The rasterizer's share of a real frame is the host's, not the engine's, and the live demo carries the Earth constellation smoothly at sixty times speed. The recording context stands in for the walk, not the pixels, which is why the browser holds the qualitative half and the node bench holds the quantitative one.
