# PHASE-4: Interoperability and platform breadth

Subject: open the engine outward. The core, adapters, and live-state work make Argelander a self-contained pipeline over its own spaceborne providers; this phase lets foreign coverage in, lets Argelander coverage out in the formats other tools read, broadens the platform class beyond satellites, and grows the instrument fixtures into a real cited catalog. The motivation and the seam-by-seam analysis that seeded it are in `docs/integrations/hyplan.md`, the study of HyPlan (an Apache-2.0 airborne and satellite mission planner); nothing here takes a code dependency on it, every path is a versioned data format.

Deliver: the interchange formats of ADR-0011, standard GeoJSON for universal lossy interop and MMGIS Enhanced GeoJSON for lossless render-host-native round trips, with strip-to-format exporters beside the AGE-14 raster and H3 aggregates and a foreign-strip reader that ingests externally produced coverage and renders it through the existing Leaflet and Three adapters (the reader reserves the `coord_properties` it models, epoch, state, quality, and passes every other producer column through untouched); a `GeoJsonStateProvider` reading platform positions as a sibling to the CZML and pre-sampled providers (AGE-04), the enhanced form supplying the time tagging plain GeoJSON lacks; generic non-satellite platforms made first-class through an airborne flight-line or trajectory `StateProvider` that emits body-fixed states so any moving platform (aircraft first, then UAV, balloon, ship, or ground vehicle) renders footprint strips through the identical `trackStrip` pipeline (the engine already treats the platform as an above-surface point, so no sampler changes), with honest handling of the loiter and hover degeneracy where the along-track velocity vanishes, the cross-track direction is undefined, and `trackStrip` today throws `RangeError` on the parallel state vectors; and a real instrument catalog as data (AGE-18), the 21 demonstration fixtures grown into a cited library of actual instruments seeded from HyPlan's optics catalog (AVIRIS, HyTES, PRISM, MASTER, UAVSAR, LVIS) and its satellite swath registry, contributed as JSON models, not code.

Exit: a coverage document produced outside Argelander round-trips through MMGIS Enhanced GeoJSON and renders identically, and its unmodeled `coord_properties` survive the round trip byte-for-byte; a strip exports to standard GeoJSON that opens in an unmodified GIS with its provenance intact; an airborne provider renders a flight-line footprint strip in the demo beside the satellite tracks; the catalog carries real, source-cited instruments as fixtures the atlas and demo draw from rather than illustrative anchors; and the charter holds throughout, `argelander-core` still ships zero runtime dependencies, the frozen strip schema does not move, and no path introduces propagation, orbit determination, or frames math. Contract-defining additions (a new provider that fixes a wire shape, a catalog manifest format) each land with their own ADR before implementation.
