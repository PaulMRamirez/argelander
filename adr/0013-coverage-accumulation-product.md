# ADR-0013: the coverage accumulation product, a look-count raster and a gap GeoJSON

Status: proposed, on hold. Date: 2026-07-13.

Parked before implementation. The design below is drafted but not being pursued: the coverage-accumulation direction may diverge from the original intent, and the open forks (per-strip versus per-footprint look count, and a latitude and longitude raster versus H3) want a clearer decision before code exists. Revive by resolving those and moving to accepted, or supersede.

## Decision

Coverage accumulation over one or more strips (AGE-14) produces a zero-dependency latitude and longitude look-count raster plus a gap GeoJSON, at the providers layer. The raster is a manifest: the common body, a geographic bounding box, a cell size in degrees, the column and row counts, and a row-major look-count array whose value at a cell is the number of input strips whose ribbon covers that cell. Coverage, overlap, and gap are thresholds on that one field, not separate arrays: a cell is covered when the look count is above zero, an overlap when it is two or more, a gap when it is zero. So the raster is the single accumulation product and the three named layers derive from it, which also makes them consistent by construction (a cell cannot be both covered and a gap). The product carries one provenance record per input strip, the AGE-14 metadata: instrument, mission, mode, the pass or encounter id, and the strip's aggregate quality ranges (the widest resolution and incidence ranges and the largest look count across its segments, the same aggregation the standard-GeoJSON export uses). The gaps also serialize as a standard GeoJSON `FeatureCollection` of the zero-count cells, run-length merged into rectangles per grid row so the polygon count stays reasonable, carrying the same provenance at the feature level, for any GIS.

The accumulation itself is pure arithmetic and adds no dependency: a strip's ribbon is the union of its connected segment quads projected to geographic, and rasterizing is a point-in-polygon test of each grid cell center against each quad over the quad's bounding box, unwrapped across the antimeridian the way the Leaflet adapter unwraps a ribbon. Look count is per strip, not per segment: a strip covers a cell zero or one times regardless of how many of its footprints fall there, so the count is the number of independent looks (passes) over the cell, which is the quantity AGE-14 names and the quantity that reads as overlap.

H3 aggregates, the other product AGE-14 names, are deferred. An H3 cell index needs an h3 library, a runtime dependency that would be its own ADR under the zero-dependency charter, whereas the latitude and longitude raster is arithmetic over arrays. The raster can be re-binned to H3 later without changing the accumulation, so nothing here forecloses it. The frozen strip schema does not move: this reads strips and emits a new product type beside them, it does not extend the strip.

## Rationale

AGE-14 names three coverage products, raster and H3 aggregates and GeoJSON outlines; the GeoJSON strip outlines are ADR-0011, and this ADR fixes the raster and the gap GeoJSON and defers H3. The latitude and longitude raster is the honest zero-dependency starting point, because rasterizing a ribbon the engine already produced and counting how many strips cover a cell is geometric accumulation, not a new intercept or an analysis-grade computation, so it stays on the charter's side of that line (the same line the pluggable intercept service is on the far side of). Making look count the single field and deriving coverage, overlap, and gap as thresholds, rather than storing three parallel masks, keeps the product small and internally consistent and matches the way PHASE-2 frames it: overlap is the multiply-covered complement of the gaps, read off the look count.

The alternatives. H3 first was declined because it front-loads a runtime dependency for a product whose value does not need one, and the charter and ADR-0009's provider-growth posture both push a new dependency to its own decision. A per-footprint or per-segment count was declined in favor of per-strip look count: a strip's own along-track footprint overlap is a rendering-grade artifact of the sampling cadence, not an independent look, so counting it would inflate overlap with self-coverage; the independent-looks reading is what a coverage analyst and AGE-14 mean by look count. Storing separate coverage, overlap, and gap arrays was declined as redundant and desynchronization-prone when all three are a threshold away from the one field. Contouring the gaps into minimal polygons (a marching-squares boundary) was declined for now in favor of per-row rectangle merging, which is exact for a raster gap, dependency-free, and enough for a GIS overlay; a smoother boundary is a later refinement that does not change the raster.

The charter holds. The accumulation and both serializers live in the providers layer, so `argelander-core` keeps its zero runtime dependencies; no propagation, orbit determination, or frames math enters, since the ribbon is already body-fixed geographic and the rasterizer is point-in-polygon arithmetic; and the products are additive around the frozen strip, which they read and never widen. The rendering-grade boundary discipline the SGP4 and interchange work set applies: the raster is a spherical latitude and longitude binning, disclosed as such on the product, not a projected equal-area grid, so a consumer that needs equal-area area totals re-bins rather than trusting a cell to be a fixed number of square kilometers.
