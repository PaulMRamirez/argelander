# ADR-0008: argelander-providers: standalone state providers below the seam

Status: proposed. Date: 2026-07-11.

## Decision

A new workspace package `argelander-providers` hosts the Phase 1 standalone StateProviders named by SPEC-PROVIDER section 2: `Sgp4Provider` (SGP4/TLE) and `PresampledProvider` (pre-sampled states with CSV ingestion), plus a port-marshalling pair (`serveStateProvider`, `remoteStateProvider`) that places any provider behind a Web Worker or MessagePort boundary with transferable flat batches, so propagation runs off the main thread (AGE-05) without the engine knowing. Its only runtime dependency is `argelander-core`; nothing external. The hard non-goals bind the engine above the seam: providers are the things that compute states, and this package is where that computation lives so that `argelander-core` stays propagation-free and zero-dependency. The npm name `argelander-providers` extends the ADR-0005 registry claim; placeholder publish follows acceptance.

SGP4 is implemented from source (the Vallado near-earth branch, WGS-72 constants), not taken as a dependency. Correctness is anchored to the published verification corpus of "Revisiting Spacetrack Report #3" (satellites 00005 and 06251, position agreement at the meter-and-below level) plus internal-consistency properties. Deep-space elements (period 225 minutes or longer) refuse at construction with a structured `DeepSpaceUnsupportedError` until SDP4 lands with its own vectors; the Phase 1 demonstrations are near-earth (SAR constellation, ICESat-2) and the Mars pair arrives pre-sampled, so the deferral costs nothing on the phase critical path.

Rendering-grade frame and time policy, recorded here because it is the analog of the "analytic ellipsoid only" non-goal: the SGP4 provider answers `TEME` natively and `ITRF93` or `IAU_EARTH` through the IAU 1982 GMST spin alone, with UT1 approximated by UTC (a configurable delta-UT1 offset is accepted) and Et taken as TT over a static leap-second table. Neglected terms are bounded and small against footprint widths: equation of the equinoxes about 35 m, polar motion about 10 m, UT1 minus UTC at most 0.9 s or about 420 m of Earth rotation at the equator. Analysis-grade intercepts stay delegated per ADR-0002. `correction` must be `'NONE'`; anything else refuses rather than mislabeling provenance. `lightTimes` carries the geometric distance over c. Coverage is the TLE fence of SPEC-PROVIDER section 4, seven days either side of the element epoch by default and configurable; epochs outside refuse atomically with `CoverageRefusalError`, and oversized queries refuse with the ceiling named in the message. `orientation()` serves Earth spin quaternions (`TEME` into `ITRF93`, SPICE scalar-first) and refuses spacecraft attitude, which TLEs do not carry; mount and scan-law composition stay above the seam.

The pre-sampled provider serves per-body tables (body, observer, frame, correction, strictly increasing epochs, flat states, optional light times) by cubic Hermite interpolation over position and velocity, refusing extrapolation with the table span as the covered window, and refusing frame, observer, or correction mismatches instead of relabeling. Optional quaternion tables serve `orientation()` by slerp. `parsePresampledCsv` ingests the `et,x,y,z,vx,vy,vz[,lt]` column format; CZML ingestion belongs to the CZML playback provider later in Phase 1 and is out of scope here.

## Rationale

A dependency (satellite.js) was considered and declined: the from-source propagator keeps the workspace free of external supply chain, writes directly into the wire `Float64Array` blocks of the frozen batch layout instead of converting per-sample objects, and the public verification corpus makes equivalence checkable to the meter, which is the same standard a dependency would be held to. Placing providers in their own package rather than in core preserves the core zero-dependency rule and the non-goal boundary while keeping the Phase 3 convergence diff small: a live Bessel binding is one more provider in this package, against the identical seam.
