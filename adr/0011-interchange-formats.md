# ADR-0011: coverage and state interchange via GeoJSON, MMGIS Enhanced GeoJSON, and CZML

Status: proposed. Date: 2026-07-12.

## Decision

Argelander supports three interchange formats for getting state in and coverage in and out, all at the providers and adapter layer, none of them a change to the frozen strip contract and none of them a runtime dependency in `argelander-core`. Standard GeoJSON (RFC 7946) is the universal, lossy interop path: a strip exports to a `FeatureCollection` of footprint outline polygons carrying the AGE-14 provenance at the feature level (mission, instrument, mode, pass or encounter id, quality ranges), readable by any GIS, and a plain GeoJSON of platform positions imports as a `StateProvider`. MMGIS Enhanced GeoJSON is the lossless, render-host-native path: it names per-coordinate members through a `coord_properties` array on the `FeatureCollection` (feature-level override allowed), so a strip's per-segment metadata rides on the coordinates themselves rather than being flattened to one set of feature properties. CZML keeps the role it already has, an inbound state provider (`argelander-providers/src/czml.ts`, ADR-0009), with a strip-to-CZML export deferred to a Cesium-hosting phase and out of scope here.

MMGIS Enhanced GeoJSON is the load-bearing choice, because MMGIS is Argelander's render host (LithoSphere on the MMGIS Globe, CLAUDE.md seam 3) and the format was built for exactly this shape: spacecraft, rover, and aircraft tracks whose coordinates carry context. Its `coord_properties` requires `longitude`, `latitude`, `elevation` as the first three members and then names any further members positionally, so Argelander reserves the names it understands and passes the rest through untouched. The reserved names are `event_seconds` for the segment epoch (`etSec` as a Unix second, the format's own time convention), `state` for the acquisition state (`planned`, `acquiring`, `committed`), and the quality members (`resolution_m`, `incidence_deg`, `look_count`) matching the frozen `QualityRange`. Any additional `coord_properties` a producer attached (a rover yaw, a pose uncertainty, an instrument gain) are neither read nor dropped: they survive a round trip through Argelander unmodified, which is the whole reason to accept the enhanced form rather than only standard GeoJSON. An Argelander strip serializes both ways: to a lossy GeoJSON outline for interop, and to a lossless Enhanced GeoJSON whose left and right edge coordinates carry the segment epoch, state, and quality, so a consumer reconstructs the exact strip.

The three roles this serves, all above or beside the frozen seam. Inbound state (AGE-04): a `GeoJsonStateProvider` reading platform positions, where the enhanced form supplies the time tagging plain GeoJSON lacks (a bare `LineString` has no epoch), doing only analytic geodetic-to-ECEF like the sibling providers, no propagation. Inbound coverage: a foreign-strip reader that ingests externally produced coverage (the strongest path in `docs/integrations/hyplan.md`), lossless from Enhanced GeoJSON, minimal (outline only, no per-segment state) from plain GeoJSON. Outbound coverage (AGE-14): the strip-to-GeoJSON and strip-to-Enhanced-GeoJSON exporters beside the raster and H3 aggregates. The strip schema (`schemas/strip.schema.json`) does not move; interchange is a serialization that maps to and from the strip, not an extension of it, so Phase 0 stays frozen and ADR-0010 remains the last additive schema evolution.

## Rationale

The requirements already reach for this: AGE-04 lists "CZML or GeoJSON interchange" among the ephemeris inputs and AGE-14 names "GeoJSON strip outlines" among the export products, so the seam was anticipated; what was undecided was whether standard GeoJSON alone suffices. It does not. Standard GeoJSON attaches properties only at the feature level, so a strip flattened to one polygon loses the per-segment epoch, state, and quality that are the substance of time-tagged coverage; you can split every segment into its own feature to recover them, but that multiplies the file by the segment count and still cannot express that two coordinates of one edge belong to different clock readings. The choice was standard GeoJSON only (rejected: lossy in exactly the dimension the engine exists to represent, and unable to round-trip a producer's own metadata), a bespoke Argelander GeoJSON extension (rejected: the render host already defines one, and inventing a second dialect of the same idea is the kind of duplication ADR-0002 warns against in the SPICE case, applied to formats), or GeoJSON plus MMGIS Enhanced GeoJSON (chosen: universal interop where lossiness is acceptable, lossless render-host-native interchange where it is not, no new format invented).

Passing unknown `coord_properties` through rather than dropping them is deliberate and is the feature the enhanced form buys. A strip that originates in MMGIS carrying rover pose or aircraft attitude, flows through Argelander for rendering, and returns to MMGIS must come back with that context intact; Argelander is a rendering-grade coverage engine, not the authority on pose, so silently discarding members it does not model would corrupt a document it was only asked to visualize. Reserving a small set of names and preserving the rest keeps Argelander an honest pass-through for the columns it does not own while still reading the ones it does.

The charter holds on every count. The serializers and the readers live in `argelander-providers` and the render adapters, so `argelander-core` keeps its zero runtime dependencies; the frozen strip contract is untouched because interchange maps to the strip rather than widening it; and no format here implies propagation, orbit determination, or frames math, since GeoJSON and Enhanced GeoJSON coordinates are already body-fixed geographic and a GeoJSON state provider performs only the same analytic geodetic-to-ECEF the CZML and pre-sampled providers already perform. The one boundary discipline the SGP4 and CZML providers already set applies here too: the epoch and frame conventions of an imported document (Unix seconds in `event_seconds`, the geographic datum, geodetic versus geocentric latitude) are recorded on the strip provenance, never hidden, so a confidently mislabeled input renders as a disclosed approximation rather than a silent error.
