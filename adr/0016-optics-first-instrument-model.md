# ADR-0016: the optics-first instrument model

Status: accepted. Date: 2026-07-13.

## Context

The instrument catalog stores derived ground geometry as the datum and exiles the intrinsic optics and the provenance into a prose note. `aviris-3.json` carries `swathHalfWidthKm: 7.2` as its parameter and buries `39.6 deg cross-track FOV, 1234 px (ORNL DAAC AV3_L1B)` in a free-text `sourceNote`; `uavsar-lband.json` carries derived `nearRangeKm` and `farRangeKm` and prose-notes the real `22-65 deg incidence`; every airborne model does the same. The swath is a value frozen at a nominal altitude stated only in the prose, and the field of view, the incidence angles, the scan half-angle, the beam divergence, and the citation, the quantities that actually define the instrument, are text a program cannot read.

The platform-and-payload study (`docs/what-the-engine-must-know.md`) concluded that this is the wrong shape. Every established sensor-geometry model that separates cleanly, the SPICE Instrument Kernel, the ISO 19130 Physical Sensor Model, OGC SensorML, STK's sensor patterns, and HyPlan's airborne instruments, stores the intrinsic collecting geometry and derives the ground geometry on demand, and none store a swath scalar as the primary datum, because a pre-derived swath is a materialized view correct at exactly one altitude and one look. Section 6 of the study named the correction (store intrinsics, derive geometry, carry the derived form only as a cache) and section 7 named the taxonomy (composable primitives with the families as presets). AGE-18 requires models as data, yet the catalog has no loader and no manifest; it is imported file by file, ad hoc. This ADR sets the optics-first direction and the contract additions it needs. It moves neither the frozen strip schema nor, in its first step, the frozen instrument-model required-parameter table; the derivations it adds are the rendering-grade analytic sphere intersection the engine already performs, not analysis-grade geometry or frames math (ADR-0014).

## Decision

The rules, numbered because they define a direction and its contract.

1. Intrinsic collecting geometry is canonical; derived ground geometry is computed. A model carries the intrinsic optics of its modality (a cross-track field of view and detector count for an imaging scanner, a near and far incidence angle and look side for a side-looker, a scan half-angle and beam divergence for a scanning lidar, and the equivalents for the other families), all in radians per the units policy, and the swath half-width, the footprint size, and the near and far ground range are derived from those together with the platform altitude, not stored as truth. Where a derived form is carried, it is a denormalized cache tagged with the altitude and pointing it was computed at, never the source.

2. The derivation is rendering grade and spherical, in `argelander-core`. A look ray at off-nadir angle theta from a platform at radius R plus h intersects the sphere of radius R analytically: the incidence follows sin(incidence) equals (R plus h) over R times sin(theta), the ground arc is R times (asin of ((R plus h) over R times sin theta) minus theta), and an intersection exists only within the limb bound. The flat-earth h times tan(theta) is the airborne small-angle fast path. This is the analytic intersection the engine already performs for edges, so it introduces no frames math and stays inside ADR-0014.

3. The derivation is altitude-parametric. The sampler reads the platform altitude from the state per segment, so an eccentric orbit or an airborne climb produces the swath that is correct along the pass rather than one baked number, which is the property a pre-derived scalar cannot express.

4. Structured provenance replaces the prose note. A model carries a structured source block (the producing source, a citation or datasheet reference, a retrieval date, and the intrinsic quantities as named fields) so that the information the `sourceNote` carries today as text becomes queryable data, the way HyPlan carries a first-class source string on its instruments. For the first step the block rides the parameters as a structured optional value, so the frozen envelope does not move.

5. Models load as JSON, with a manifest. A JSON instrument-model loader in `argelander-core` validates a document and returns `InstrumentModel` records, and a catalog manifest, an index of the models the catalog contains, lets a host load the catalog as data rather than importing files one by one (AGE-18). The manifest is a new interchange format, and it is the new contract this ADR sanctions; its shape is specified in the accompanying spec revision.

6. The taxonomy moves toward composable primitives with the families as presets. The twenty-one-family `kind` stays the public tag on the strip provenance and the authoring preset, while the model internals move toward the composable primitives the study named, the mount, the pointing mechanism, the field-of-view shape, the sampling layout, and the projection kernel, so that a new modality becomes a new preset over stable primitives rather than a new leaf type and a new code path. This is the lowest-priority and most deferrable rule, and its detailed schema is SPEC-INSTRUMENT-MODEL v2 work, not the first step.

7. Migration is additive first, and the frozen anchors do not move. The intrinsic optics and the structured provenance ride the spec's sanctioned optional-parameter extension point, and the samplers prefer them when present, deriving the geometry, and fall back to the baked derived parameter otherwise. The twenty-one conformance anchors, which are demonstration constants read from the atlas tiles rather than real instruments, and the frozen strip schema, stay unmoved while the real catalog migrates to optics and structured provenance and the atlas conformance replay still matches within tolerance. Only once the derivation is proven against the whole catalog does a SPEC-INSTRUMENT-MODEL v2 promote the optics to the required set for real instruments, retire the baked parameters, and, if adopted, land the composable-primitive taxonomy of rule 6. That v2 is the step that amends the ADR-0007 freeze of the required-parameter table; this ADR's first step does not.

## Rationale

The evidence is in the catalog itself: the notes carry the field of view, the incidence, the beam divergence, and the datasheet, which is the model, and the parameters carry a number that is only true at an altitude the note has to disclose. Storing the derived side is the one thing every reference model avoids, for the reason the study gives: altitude and pointing vary within a pass, a single swath cannot express near-far asymmetry or off-nadir growth or limb saturation, and the same intrinsic record must serve airborne and orbital where the derivation formula itself differs, so only the intrinsics are portable. Making the derivation spherical and altitude-parametric is what turns the catalog altitude-honest and dissolves the nominal-altitude disclosure the earlier review flagged, and it costs nothing in charter terms because it is the analytic intersection already in the engine. Structured provenance and a JSON loader with a manifest are what AGE-18 means by models as data, and they are missing today. The additive migration is chosen over a cutover because the conformance anchors are atlas-derived demonstration constants, not optics, so forcing them into an optics-first shape would be awkward and high risk, while riding the optional-parameter extension point reaches the same end state without moving the frozen envelope, the frozen strip schema, or the anchors until the derivation has earned it.

## Alternatives rejected

Keep the baked-swath catalog is rejected: it is a materialized view frozen at one altitude, it loses the optics to prose, and it is the shape every standard avoids. A big-bang cutover, changing the required-parameter table and regenerating the twenty-one anchors as optics in one step, is rejected as the first move: the anchors are demonstration constants read from atlas tiles, not derivable from a field of view, so forcing them optics-first is both awkward and high risk, and the additive path reaches the same end state safely. Importing HyPlan's instrument models is rejected: its flat-earth math is wrong for wide swaths at orbital altitude and its Python class hierarchy is code, not data; this ADR adopts the optics-first principle and the structured-source idea in the engine's own spherical, data-first shape. Carrying a manifest as an informal convention rather than a specified format is rejected: a catalog index other tools read is a contract, so it is specified, per the repository's own rule that a catalog manifest format lands with an ADR.

## Consequences

The catalog becomes altitude-honest and its optics become queryable data rather than prose, and the engine gains a rendering-grade optics-to-geometry derivation and a JSON loader with a manifest, which is a new interchange contract. The twenty-five catalog files are reworked to carry intrinsic optics and structured provenance, the samplers gain the derivation path, and a SPEC-INSTRUMENT-MODEL v2 follows to promote the optics to required and, if adopted, land the composable-primitive taxonomy. The frozen strip schema, the frozen required-parameter table, and the twenty-one conformance anchors are untouched during the transition, so nothing that renders today breaks. This is correction B from the study, and it precedes the documentation reframe so the examples describe the right shape; it is orthogonal to the state-source work, where OMM is done and OEM and the SPK recipe are independent of it.
