# ADR-0012: what trackStrip renders when the along-track direction vanishes

Status: proposed. Date: 2026-07-13.

## Decision

When `position x velocity` is degenerate in `trackStrip`, so the cross-track direction (the orbit normal) is undefined, the engine no longer throws. The degeneracy happens when a platform's velocity is zero or parallel to its position: a hover, a loiter, a vertical ascent, a full stop. Two cases replace the throw. When a prior segment in the same batch carried a valid cross-track direction, the degenerate segment carries that last valid direction forward and renders as an ordinary segment, its edges and any swath sub-structure drawn at the held orientation; this is the mid-track hover, the common case. When no prior valid direction exists, so the track begins degenerate with the platform stationary at its first sample, the segment collapses to a zero-width stare: its left and right edges are both the nadir ground point and it carries no cross-track sub-structure. The position-at-origin guard, a platform at the body center, stays a thrown `RangeError`, because that is nonsense rather than a physical state.

## Rationale

The throw was a data-quality guard written when every platform was a satellite, where `position x velocity` is never degenerate because an orbiting body always moves across its own radius vector. PHASE-4 broadens the platform class to aircraft, UAVs, balloons, ships, and ground vehicles, and for those a vanishing along-track velocity is a legitimate physical state, a hover or a loiter or a stop, not necessarily bad data. Rendering it is more honest than refusing it, and refusing it would make the whole non-satellite platform class unrenderable the moment it paused.

Carrying the last valid cross-track direction forward is the rendering-grade choice for a moving platform that stops. The swath keeps the orientation it had, so a brief pause reads as continued coverage of the same ground rather than a gap or a flipped ribbon. This holds exactly the information the degenerate state lost (the along-track heading) from the last moment it was known, which is the best available estimate while the platform is stationary. Carrying a direction backward from a future valid segment was declined as more surprising than a forward hold and unnecessary for the realistic case, where a platform is moving before it pauses.

The zero-width stare for a track that begins degenerate is the honest fallback where there is genuinely no orientation to hold. It is a point at the nadir ground position, and it carries a single bead there so the existing sparse path renders it as a dot. A bare zero-width segment would draw nothing: the sparse painter is gated on a segment carrying sub-structure and the swath paths skip zero width, so the bead is what makes the stare visible, through machinery that already exists (the bead and event sparse rendering) with no adapter change. The connection rule in the Leaflet adapter refuses to ribbon a zero-width segment (it gates on both neighbors having positive width), so the stare stays a lone point rather than blending into a swath. It is also consistent with how the engine already handles a related degeneracy: `crossTrackRotationRad` folds an undefined local east to zero at the poles rather than failing, the same rendering-grade posture of degrading to a disclosed default instead of throwing.

The cost is that a genuinely corrupt state, a provider emitting parallel vectors by mistake, now renders as a stare or a held-orientation segment rather than failing loudly. This is accepted because the physical case is now legitimate, so the engine cannot tell a real hover from a bad sample without overreaching into provider validation that the charter keeps out of core; a rendering-grade engine should degrade to a disclosed point rather than throw, and a provider that wants to reject its own bad data can do so before the states reach `trackStrip`. The position-magnitude guard stays because a body-center position has no nadir direction at all, so there is nothing to render and nothing to hold.
