# What the engine must know: platform and payload

A first-principles study of the information an acquisition geometry engine must hold about the platform and the payload to render footprint strips across platform classes, from orbital spacecraft to suborbital sounding rockets to stratospheric balloons to aircraft. The purpose is to derive the required model from the physics of the problem, check the derived shape against established practice (NASA SPICE, ISO 19130, OGC SensorML, STK, CCSDS) and against the two working examples this repository already touches (Argelander itself and HyPlan), and decide what the multi-class goal actually requires. This is a study, not a contract. Where it recommends changing a frozen contract, that change is an ADR and a spec revision of its own; nothing here moves a seam.

## 1. The geometric kernel

Reduce a footprint to its irreducible core. It is the intersection of an instrument line of sight with a body surface, and at rendering grade the surface is an analytic ellipsoid held static in a body-fixed frame. So a single ground point is the solution of one equation: cast a ray from an origin, in a direction, against a fixed ellipsoid. Everything the engine must know is whatever locates that origin, orients that direction, and guarantees that origin, direction, and surface share one frame at one instant. A footprint strip adds a fourth demand, because it is a time-ordered sequence: the engine must know enough kinematics to place successive rays and to know how far the scene moved under the instrument between them. The rest of this study is an exhaustive decomposition against those four demands, split into what the platform supplies and what the payload supplies.

## 2. The platform: what must be known, and why

Four items, each with a distinct and separable reason. The separation matters, because conflating them is the classic modeling error.

Position is the ray origin, and through the platform height above the ellipsoid it also sets the footprint scale, since a fixed angular field subtends a ground area that grows with slant range. Position has no substitute and no degeneracy: it is always required and always well defined.

Velocity is not needed to intersect one ray at one instant; that computation needs only origin, direction, and surface. Velocity re-enters for the strip, and for four reasons that should not be fused: the along-track basis of an assumed pointing frame, which cannot be built from position alone (position gives the local vertical, velocity gives the second axis, which is exactly how Orekit builds attitude from a date-plus-position-velocity state and how STK constrains a nadir-aligned vehicle X axis to the velocity vector); the along-track sample spacing that separates successive cross-track segments on the ground; motion smear over a finite integration time; and stellar aberration, which depends on observer velocity even though light time does not. At rendering grade with geometric correction the aberration reason is dormant, but the other three are load-bearing, and velocity is the item most prone to degenerating to a meaningless near-zero vector.

Attitude is the ray direction, the platform orientation composed with a fixed mount and a scan law. Three things must be pinned that are commonly conflated: the rotation itself, a quaternion taking a reference frame into the platform body frame, which is the SPICE C-matrix concept exactly; the reference frame the attitude is stated against, which differs by class and mode, local-orbital for an Earth-observation satellite, local-geodetic for an aircraft or balloon gimbal, and inertial for a sounding-rocket science payload that stares at a star or the Sun; and whether the attitude is measured, assumed, or unknown, which is a data-quality axis rather than a geometry axis but governs whether the footprint is trustworthy. Attitude rate is a distinct sub-item, needed for smear and fast slews, and carried explicitly in SPICE CK as the optional angular-velocity component.

Time is the common instant. The three evaluators, platform state, platform attitude, and body orientation, must be read at the same physical instant or the footprint lands in the wrong place, and the requirement is not a timestamp but one continuous leap-free unambiguous scale shared by all three. The stakes are concrete: the Earth surface moves about 465 metres per second at the equator, so a one-second clock mismatch smears the footprint about 465 metres, and in 2026 the UTC to barycentric-dynamical-time offset is about 69 seconds, roughly 32 kilometres of surface error if left unconverted. Mandating a single ephemeris-time scale at the seam removes an entire bug class; the clock correlations belong below the seam.

The frame and rotation problem is who rotates the body. The surface is fixed in the body-fixed frame but the platform naturally lives in an inertial one, and something must apply the body spin model (SPICE PCK, the IAU rotation elements). The clean choices are to deliver state in inertial and rotate the ray, or to deliver state already body-fixed and intersect against a static ellipsoid. Delivering body-fixed keeps the PCK rotation entirely below the seam, so the engine performs only analytic ellipsoid intersection in a frame where the ellipsoid does not move, which is precisely the stated non-goal boundary. For self-consistency the engine must then request attitude against that same body-fixed frame, so origin, direction, and surface are all in one frame with no residual inertial rotation above the seam.

## 3. The platform classes, and where they break

The information shape is invariant across classes; only the values, the sources, and the validity vary.

| Class | Position and velocity source | Attitude source | Pointing reference in practice | Dominant degeneracy |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Orbital | GNSS or tracking reconstructed to an ephemeris; TLE and SGP4 at rendering grade; CCSDS OEM | Star tracker and gyro fused to a CK, or nadir derived from the state | Local-orbital by default, inertial or target-track when slewing | Persistent velocity, mostly well behaved; GEO and drift cases go velocity-null in the body-fixed frame |
| Suborbital | Radar or GPS trajectory, a short arc of minutes | Onboard attitude-control telemetry, sun and star sensors, often inertial and only during the science window | Inertial star or Sun stare above the atmosphere; ballistic and undefined during spin-up, coast, and descent | Velocity nulls and turns through the vertical at apogee; attitude exists only in a subinterval of the ephemeris |
| Balloon | GPS telemetry, low rate, noisy under differentiation | Gondola inertial measurement and star camera on a stabilized platform; azimuth often free | Gimbal referenced to local-geodetic or inertial, never to the wind-driven velocity | Station-keeping with near-zero groundspeed, trajectory reversal, free azimuth |
| Aircraft | GNSS and inertial blended navigation, high rate | Inertial navigation attitude, gimbal encoders for pods | Inertial attitude with crab, heading not equal to track under wind | Tight turns where bank decouples the boresight from the velocity vector |

What is common to all four is the invariant core: a time-tagged position, a velocity that must be treated with suspicion as a pointing basis, a boresight that reduces to an attitude quaternion against a named frame, a single monotonic clock, and the intersection performed body-fixed. What is genuinely class-specific is not the shape but the pointing reference frame, the motion regime, the attitude availability window, and the altitude regime. The unifying failure mode across three of the four classes is velocity-null or velocity-attitude divergence: any time the ground velocity is small or points differently from the boresight, the derive-pointing-from-the-trajectory path collapses and measured attitude becomes mandatory. Orbital platforms mostly escape this, which is exactly why nadir-assumption tooling grew up in the orbital world and why it silently breaks for the atmospheric and ballistic classes.

## 4. The payload: the acquisition chain

Given a platform state, the footprint is the payload field of view pushed through a chain of transforms and intersected with the body. Every modality is the same chain with different fillings: the mount, a rigid platform-to-instrument transform of a lever-arm offset and a fixed boresight rotation; the pointing mechanism, the only genuinely kinematic intrinsic, one of fixed, gimbal, scan-mirror, spin, or step-stare, with its axes, ranges, rates, and scan law; the collecting geometry, the pencil of rays about the boresight, a field-of-view shape and half-extents, or for side-lookers a near and far look angle, plus beam divergence and aperture; the sampling layout, how the field of view is cut into samples, cross-track and along-track counts, instantaneous field of view, beam or bead count and spacing; the timing, the line, frame, scan, or pulse rate and the integration or dwell, which converts platform motion into along-track sample spacing; and the ground projection, which intersects the boundary and sample rays with the body to yield the left and right edges, the near and far ground range, and the footprint size.

The load-bearing distinction is intrinsic versus derived. Intrinsic characteristics are fixed physical properties of the optics, antenna, and mount that do not change when the platform moves: field of view and field of regard, boresight and mounting, scan half-angle and scan law, look and incidence angles, beam divergence, aperture, detector and beam count, instantaneous field of view, frame and scan and pulse rates, integration time, bandwidth, wavelength. Derived ground geometry is everything that exists only once altitude and pointing are supplied: swath half-width, near and far ground range, footprint size, ground sample distance, off-nadir footprint growth. The intrinsic set is a property of the box; the derived set is a property of the box at a place. A single pre-computed swath scalar is a derived quantity frozen at one altitude and one look, wrong the instant either changes.

## 5. The ground projection, and why it must be spherical

The rendering-grade primitive is a look ray at off-nadir angle theta from a platform at radius R plus h, intersected with a sphere of radius R. Solving the triangle of body center, platform, and ground point gives the incidence angle from the identity sin(incidence) equals (R plus h) over R times sin(theta), so the incidence always exceeds the look angle by the earth-curvature term; the earth-central angle to the ground point is alpha equals asin of ((R plus h) over R times sin theta) minus theta; the ground range along the arc is R times alpha; and an intersection exists only for sin(theta) at most R over (R plus h), the maximal look angle grazing the limb. A swath is this evaluated at the two field-of-view edges, and the footprint of one sample of angular width equal to the instantaneous field of view is the difference of the ground arcs across that width, which is the exact off-nadir growth.

The flat-earth shortcut, ground range equals h times tan(theta), is what airborne planners use deliberately, and it agrees with the spherical result to well within rendering tolerance at aircraft altitudes and modest angles. It breaks in three regimes an orbital engine must handle: wide swath, where the divergence from the true arc becomes kilometres across the swath; near the limb, where h times tan(theta) runs to infinity while the true ground range saturates at the horizon; and limb or occultation sounders, where the boresight never meets the surface and the flat model is undefined. The rule for the engine is that airborne and small-angle nadir may take flat-earth as a fast path, but the canonical projection is the spherical intersection, and wide-field or orbital modalities must use it.

## 6. The storage judgment: store intrinsics, derive geometry

Every established sensor-geometry model that separates cleanly stores the intrinsic optics and derives the ground geometry, and none store a swath scalar as the primary datum. A SPICE Instrument Kernel holds a boresight, a reference frame, and a field-of-view shape of circle, ellipse, rectangle, or polygon with its boundary given as angles or as corner rays, and it deliberately holds no swath and no altitude; the state comes from other kernels. ISO 19130 formalizes the same split as a Physical Sensor Model of the physics and geometry alongside the minimum support data needed to geolocate. OGC SensorML decomposes a sensor into position, pointing, and detector as stages of a process chain. STK stores a sensor pattern as an intrinsic angular shape and computes the ground projection against the central body on demand. HyPlan, the airborne example, stores field of view and across-track pixels and derives instantaneous field of view, ground sample distance, and swath, stores near and far incidence angles and derives swath and near and far ground distances, and stores scan half-angle and beam divergence and derives density and footprint; it never stores a pre-derived swath scalar as truth.

The judgment for this engine is the same, and the reasons are load-bearing rather than stylistic. Altitude and pointing vary within a single pass, so any pre-derived swath is correct at one instant and wrong elsewhere. A single swath scalar cannot express near-far asymmetry, off-nadir footprint growth, or limb saturation, all of which are first-class rendering features here, because the derivation is a function of the look angle rather than a number. And the same intrinsic record must serve airborne with the flat-earth fast path and orbital with the spherical kernel, where the derivation formula itself differs, so only the intrinsics are portable across that boundary. The derived ground geometry is still worth carrying, but strictly as a denormalized cache on the emitted strip, tagged with the altitude and pointing it was computed at, useful for level-of-detail selection and labeling and never the source of truth. Intrinsics are the model; derived geometry is a materialized view.

## 7. The taxonomy judgment: composable primitives, families as presets

The roughly twenty geometry families should not be a flat closed enum of distinct types. They are better modeled as a small set of composable primitives, exactly the intrinsic blocks of section 4, with each named family a preset over that primitive space, exposed as a discriminated union whose tag selects the projection kernel and validates the legal combinations. The primitives are the mount, the pointing mechanism in fixed, gimbal, scan-mirror, spin, or step-stare, the field-of-view shape in cone, rectangle, ellipse, polygon, or side-look near and far, the sampling layout, the timing, and the projection kernel in panoramic-flat, spherical-intersect, side-look-range, conical-constant-incidence, tangent-limb, or fixed-grid. The evidence that this is right is the collapse across families: pushbroom, push-frame, multi-angle, whiskbroom, cross-track sounder, and geostationary raster differ only in pointing and sampling over the spherical-intersect kernel, and stripmap, ScanSAR, TOPS, spotlight, and SweepSAR differ only in pointing schedule and sub-swath count over the side-look-range kernel. Six projection kernels crossed with the composable primitives cover all twenty-one families with no gaps, and also cover instruments that fit no single named family, such as a gimballed multi-beam scanning lidar or an agile framing camera. The family list stays, but as the public interface and the test taxonomy, the family tag on the strip provenance, the authoring presets, and the atlas conformance corpus, not as the internal representation. A fixed leaf enum forces every new modality into a new leaf type and a new code path, whereas a preset over stable primitives forces only a new tag and at most one new kernel; SweepSAR was such a novelty once and the next digital-beamforming or agile mode will be too.

## 8. Convergence: the same shape as Argelander, HyPlan, or different

The derived model matches the two examples on different axes, and the answer is not uniform.

| Axis | Derived ideal | Argelander today | HyPlan |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| State seam | Two calls, ephemeris-shaped state and attitude-shaped orientation, body-fixed on request, one dynamical clock, coverage and refusal | This exactly (SPEC-PROVIDER) | No clean seam; propagation inline and per-class trajectory code |
| Instrument storage | Intrinsic optics canonical, derived geometry a cache on the strip | Stores derived geometry as the family parameters | Stores intrinsic optics and derives |
| Derivation math | Spherical intersection | Has the spherical intersection for edges, does not derive swath from optics and altitude | Flat-earth tangent |
| Taxonomy | Composable primitives, families as presets | A closed family enum, but with a composable mount chain already | Per-class code hierarchy, not data |

On the platform state seam, the engine converges on Argelander, because Argelander converged on the SPICE and CCSDS separation of ephemeris from attitude; HyPlan has no such seam. On the instrument model the ideal is a third shape, neither Argelander's nor HyPlan's: Argelander stores the derived side, which every standard avoids, while HyPlan stores the right side but with flat-earth math and a code hierarchy that violates models-as-data. Yet Argelander's envelope, its family kind and its composable mount and its timing and validity, is structurally the closest thing to the ideal spine; the correction is to change what lives in the parameters from derived geometry to intrinsic optics, add the projection kernels, and read the families as presets over the primitives rather than as a closed leaf set.

## 9. The finding beneath the convergence

The engine today derives pointing as the unit of the cross product of position and velocity, the orbital-world construction that assumes nadir and takes the cross-track direction from the velocity. This collapses for exactly the classes the multi-class goal names: at rocket apogee the velocity nulls and turns through the vertical, a station-keeping balloon has near-zero wind-driven groundspeed and a free azimuth, and a banking aircraft's boresight leaves the velocity vector. The degenerate-track policy that carries the last valid cross forward or emits a zero-width stare is a patch over the symptom, not the fix. The fix the whole community uses is measured attitude, and for these classes attitude is inertially referenced and intermittent, real only in a rocket's science window, taken from a balloon's star camera rather than its track. Supporting suborbital and balloon honestly is therefore less a state-format question than an attitude question, and the specific gap is the epistemic envelope around attitude that the seam cannot currently state: an attitude source and quality tag distinguishing measured from assumed from unknown, an attitude coverage window and refusal symmetric to the state coverage the seam already advertises, an attitude angular rate for smear during coning and pendulation, and explicit semantics for when the velocity is unreliable as a pointing basis. These are additive to the attitude call and touch neither the state batch nor the geometric core.

## 10. What this study recommends

Two corrections follow, both of them contract-and-spec work rather than one-off code, and both of them upstream of adding more state formats.

Correction A, on the provider seam, is the attitude epistemic envelope of section 9, added as additive facets in the manner the seam already used for its instance identity and its coverage advertisement, checked against the Bessel contract the seam is a subset of, so that measured attitude becomes first-class and velocity-derived pointing stops being the silent default. Correction B, on the instrument model, is the optics-first shape of sections 6 and 7: keep the envelope of kind, mount, timing, and validity, change the parameters from derived ground geometry to intrinsic optics, add the spherical projection kernels, let the derived geometry live as a cache on the strip, and read the families as presets over composable primitives. Each is its own ADR and its own spec revision.

What this study deliberately does not recommend is a redesign. The strip product, the two-call state seam, the body-fixed-on-request frame discipline, the composable mount, the family list as an interface, the rendering-grade and precision-deferred charter, and the refusal to embed a second SPICE are all confirmed by first principles and by prior art; the corrections are targeted. Nor does it recommend importing the mission-planning calculus that surrounds these intrinsics in a planner like HyPlan, the contiguity solvers, motion-blur and stereo-accuracy models, and coverage-rate and revisit search; that is a flight planner's job and it is what the intercept-service seam and the analysis-grade boundary already defer.

The roadmap consequence is the decision this study exists to inform. Because orbital, suborbital, and balloon support is gated on corrections A and B rather than on more inbound formats, and because the state-source phase would otherwise build its examples and its documentation on the shapes these corrections change, the question for the phase plan is whether to proceed with the state-source build as written, or to resequence it behind the attitude and instrument-model ADRs. This study's position is that the two corrections should land first, or interleave, so the breadth the state work advertises is real rather than geometrically plausible and epistemically overconfident.
